About The Temple

A Sanctuary of Faith &
Dravidian Architecture

The Temple of God Muruga in Puducherry stands as a testament to centuries of devotion. Built in the traditional Dravidian style, the temple features a towering Rajagopuram adorned with intricate sculptures depicting the legends of Skanda.

Devotees flock here to seek the blessings of Lord Muruga, the god of war and victory, for courage, wisdom, and prosperity. The sanctum sanctorum radiates a powerful divine energy that calms the mind and uplifts the soul.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the Darshan timings? expand_more
The temple is open daily from 5:30 AM to 12:30 PM and 4:00 PM to 9:00 PM. Special Darshan timings may vary during festival days.
Is there a dress code for entry? expand_more
Yes, traditional attire is requested. Men are encouraged to wear dhotis or trousers, and women to wear sarees or churidars. Shorts and sleeveless tops are not permitted.
How do I receive my booked tickets? expand_more
Once your payment is confirmed, e-tickets will be sent to your registered email address and mobile number via SMS. You can also view them in the 'My Bookings' section.
Are cameras allowed inside? expand_more
Photography is permitted in the outer praharams (corridors). However, photography is strictly prohibited inside the main sanctum sanctorum.
